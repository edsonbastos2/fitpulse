# ADR-002: Guided First Workout Onboarding Approach

## Status

Accepted

## Date

2026-04-13

## Context

FitPulse targets beginners as its primary V1 audience. Market data shows 40% of users abandon workout apps due to logging complexity. After registration, users are currently redirected to `/profile/setup` (a non-existent route). The dashboard's "Novo Treino" button links to `/workouts/create` (also non-existent). Without a guided first experience, beginners face decision paralysis and churn before completing their first workout.

## Decision

Adopt the **Guided First Workout** approach: after registration, users see a quick 30-second tour presenting 3 starter templates in pt-BR, choose one, and immediately execute their first session with guided logging. The "Novo Treino" button on the dashboard also leads to the template-vs-blank choice screen rather than directly to the workout builder.

## Alternatives Considered

### Alternative 1: Workout Builder First

- **Description**: Primary flow is creating a workout from scratch; templates are secondary. Users see an empty workout list with CTA to build.
- **Pros**: Maximum flexibility; suits users who already have a workout routine
- **Cons**: High friction for beginners (40% churn risk); decision paralysis; requires user to know exercises, sets, reps before first use
- **Why rejected**: Target audience is beginners — they need guidance, not blank canvases

### Alternative 2: Dashboard-Driven Onboarding

- **Description**: No dedicated onboarding flow. Dashboard cards guide users organically: "Start with a ready-made workout", "Create your own", "Explore exercises".
- **Pros**: No forced onboarding; respects user autonomy
- **Cons**: No direction = higher abandonment; requires 3+ different experiences to maintain; dilutes the primary activation metric
- **Why rejected**: Paradox of choice — too many paths reduce activation rate

## Consequences

### Positive

- Maximizes the 60% workout activation KPI (first workout within 7 days)
- Directly addresses the #1 churn driver (complexity) with a guided path
- Templates in pt-BR reduce cognitive load for Brazilian users
- Creates a repeatable onboarding pattern that scales with additional templates in V2

### Negative

- Users who already know what they want must click through the template screen (extra step)
- Requires building a tour/onboarding UX in addition to the core workout pages
- If the tour is too long, it becomes the new friction point

### Risks

- **Tour length**: Must be < 30 seconds or it becomes the new churn point. Mitigation: skip button always visible, max 3 template cards.
- **Template relevance**: 3 templates may not cover all beginner goals (e.g., cardio, mobility). Mitigation: clear descriptions help users understand applicability.

## Implementation Notes

- The onboarding flow lives at `/workouts/create` as a two-step screen: (1) template selection or blank, (2) workout builder pre-filled if template chosen.
- Dashboard "Novo Treino" button behavior unchanged — still navigates to `/workouts/create`.
- Tour can be a simple card carousel with brief descriptions, not a multi-page wizard.
- First-workout flag on user profile (tracked via `user_profiles` table) determines whether to show guided flow or direct to builder on subsequent visits.
